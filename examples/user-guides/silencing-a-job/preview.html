Silencing alerts for a job

If a job is going to miss its check-ins for a known reason — planned maintenance, a service upgrade, building works — you can silence it so Cronmon doesn't send alerts in the meantime. This guide walks through silencing a job and leaving a note for your colleagues explaining why.

1Log in

Go to Cronmon and sign in with your usual username and password.

2Find the job

Your jobs are listed on the home page. Type part of a job's name into the filter box to narrow the list, then click the job you want — here we're using the rotate-logs job.

3Silence the job

Click the Silenced switch. Two extra fields appear: Silenced until, which is set to 24 hours from now unless you change it, and an optional Reason. Type a short reason so your colleagues can see why alerts are off — for example, "service is being upgraded".

There's no save button — your changes take effect straight away, and Cronmon won't alert about this job until the Silenced until time passes.
